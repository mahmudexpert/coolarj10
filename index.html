Machines and Services

Fused Deposition Molding (FDM) 3D Printing

Also known as Fast Fusion Molding (FFM), this is the most common method of 3D printing. It involves melting plastic and extruding it layer by layer. The plastic dries instantaneously, creating a solid object.

  • Layer height of 0.2mm
  • Choose from a variety of colors
  • PLA and ABS plastic are strong and sturdy with some flex on thinner parts
  • Ideal for larger parts

Stereolithography (SLA) 3D Printing

SLA printing uses a laser to cure layers of liquid resin into your target object. Parts are extremely accurate, but often more brittle compared to parts printed using FDM.

  • Layer height of 0.05 to 0.1mm
  • A selection of rigid, flexible, and low friction materials
  • PLA and ABS plastic are strong and sturdy with some flex on thinner parts
  • Ideal for smaller parts, or intricate features requiring precise detail and accuracy

CNC Milling

Computer Numerical Control (CNC) Milling uses software to program toolpaths for cutting, carving, and drilling sheets of material such as metal, wood, and plastic.

  • Cut aluminum, steel, wood, plastic, carbon fiber, and more
  • Ideal for small and large parts that require the strength of metal or the look of wood, and can be cut out of a flat sheet.
  • 4th axis option allows cutting of cylindrical or multi-sided parts
